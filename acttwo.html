THE MURDER THAT NEVER WAS

Act Two

Characters

Muriel

Jane

Michael

Alex

The Police Inspector

Five people are gathered together in an old, creaky, country house.

Muriel: The Inspector seems to be taking a long time searching upstairs.

Alex: I have very little confidence in him.

Jane: Well I have, he was as mystified by the wine glass thing as I was

Michael: Mystified is right.

Alex: It's true, as far as I can see he has no more idea what is going on than the rest of us.

Jane: I think he is going to surprise us.

Muriel: He frightened me when he said there hadn't been a murder yet. Does that mean he thinks there will be one?

Jane: And does he mean it might be one of us?

Alex: One of us what? Who commits the murder or gets Murdered?

Michael: Yes good question.

Jane: And why is he taking so long?

Michael: You know what he said earlier, slowly, slowly catchee monkee

Alex: Yes he seems to have taken that to heart and just works at one speed.

Jane: Do you think one of us should go up and see if he is ok?

Murial: No Jane, he was quite adamant that he did not want us to interfere

Jane: Going up to see if he is ok is hardly interfering.

Michael: Muriel is right Jane. If we go up he will probably accuse us of contaminating the staircase.

Alex: That would not surprise me.

Jane: You are probably going to think I am a bit silly if I mention this but something has been bothering me.

Michael: Tell us anyway Jane:

Jane: Please don't moan or groan when I mention this but it is about that glass of wine

Alex and Michael: (Mockingly) Moan. Groan.

Jane: I knew you would do something like that which is why I kept quiet.

Muriel: I am interested Jane. What is it?

Michael: Yes, enlighten us on the mystery of the wine glass.

Jane: It isn't the wine glass as such but connected to it

Alex: In what way?

Jane: When Muriel first asked if we wanted a drink, I asked for a white wine and Michael asked for a scotch.

Michael: Yes I did

Jane: And did you drink it Michael?

Michael: Yes of course (pause) at least, I think I did.

Jane: I know people can be quite choosy about whisky, so was it to your taste?

Michael: I am not a connoisseur but I believe it was acceptable.

Jane: And you drank it all?

Michael: Yes, I believe I did.

Jane: So where is the glass?

Michael: Hmmph, well I don't know, I must have put it back on the drinks table. It is not a mystery.

Jane: I think it is. Muriel, you are the only person that has been near that drinks table, is there a used glass on it?

Muriel: Let me see (short pause) No, they are all clean.

Alex: Oh this is ridiculous I cannot believe we are making all this fuss about wine and whisky glasses when there is a murder inquiry going on?

Jane: But there is one more thing

Muriel: Go on.

Jane: The second time you asked us if we wanted a drink, when you spotted I had not drunk mine, Michael asked for a scotch 'If there is one'.

Alex: This really does seem rather silly.

Jane: He knew there was scotch because you had served him one previously.

Michael: With all this murder business going on I had probably forgotten.

Muriel: And also forgotten where you put the empty glass and to be honest Michael you didn't seem all that convincing when you said you'd drunk it.

Michael: Of course I drunk it. It didn't just disappear into thin air.

Muriel: Apparently it might have done.

Alex: Anyway I can't see that we need to attach any importance to it.

Jane: Well I do!

Alex: How?

Jane: Because I get the feeling somebody is playing with us

Muriel: What do you mean playing with us Jane?

Jane: I am not really sure but it seems like someone is deliberately trying to confuse us.

Muriel: Do you think it is the inspector?

Jane: I really don't know.

Alex: Well lets see what he has to say about it when he comes down.

Michael: If he comes down.

Alex: (quite loudly) Ahh inspector. Talk of the devil.

Muriel: We were wondering where you had got to.

Inspector: You were all very keen for me to be investigating and that is exactly what I have been doing.

Alex: And did you find a body.

Inspector: No sir, I did not find a body but then, I didn't expect to.

Muriel: What did you expect to find then Inspector.

Inspector: Clues miss, that is what I expected to find, clues.

Michael: And did you find any?

Inspector: Indeed I did sir but it was not without a problem.

Jane: What kind of problem?

Inspector: A crime with too few clues can be difficult to solve. It is my belief that when there are too many clues, solving the case can be even more difficult.

Michael: So just the right number of clues is essential to solving the crime is it?

Inspector: Not essential sir but it helps. And I did note your air of sarcasm.

Muriel: Don't mind Michael he is always like this.

Michael: Am I?

Muriel: Errr yess. I think so.

Alex: So no body Inspector but what clues have you unearthed?

Inspector: Clues that are surprising and intriguing.

Jane: Can I ask one thing Inspector? Has there actually been a murder?

Inspector: I am happy to say as yet, there has not.

Muriel: Pheww! Well that's a relief.

Jane: Inspector, you say 'As yet'. As if you are expecting one.

Inspector: That is true miss. I am. Or at the very least, an attempt.

Muriel: Oh no!

Alex: But who? Where?

Inspector: As for where, I believe in this very house.

Michael: And who:

Inspector: There you have me sir, I am a police officer, not a clairvoyant.

Alex: So you believe there will be a murder but no idea who it will be. Is that correct?

Inspector: That is correct sir.

Muriel: Why inspector? What makes you think someone will be murdered.

Inspector: The clues miss, because of the clues.

Alex: Of which you said, I believe, there are too many.

Inspector. Indeed I did sir.

Jane: I am curious. Is it because there are too many clues that the murder victim is difficult to spot?

Inspector: Precisely miss, You are very astute.

Alex: Can you share with us the information you have up to now Inspector?

Inspector: Yes, perhaps that would be a good idea.

Michael: As you said no body but what about the clues, what are they?

Inspector: I was coming to that sir.

Alex: I can't wait to hear.

Muriel: Nor can I.

Inspector: And so you shall. The clues I found were murder weapons?

Jane: You say weapons not just one weapon Inspector?

Inspector: Precisely.

Michael: How many?

Inspector: Four sir.

Muriel: Four! Oh my gosh that is one for each of us.(In a frightened voice) Inspector is someone planning to murder all four of us?

Inspector: Calm please, and let me explain

Michael: I think we ought to get out of here

Inspector: Please, one moment sir, there is no need for panic.

Alex: Seems like there is every need for it to me.

Inspector: If you will just let me explain

Jane: Yes please do.

Inspector: Although I found four weapons, they were not all in the same place. In fact not even in the same room.

Jane: So does that suggest anything to you inspector?

Inspector: Yes, very much so.

Alex: In what way?

Inspector: It suggests to me that they were not all going to be used by the same person.

Muriel: This is getting crazier.

Inspector: I was thinking much the same thing miss.

Michael: Do you have any idea at all what is happening inspector?

Inspector: No clear idea sir but I am working on my suspicions.

Jane: I think we really all ought to leave, and right now.

Inspector: No! Not yet. I can't allow that and at the moment it is not even possible.

Jane: Why ever not?

Inspector: You will just have to trust me for the moment because it is in your own interests that for the time being you all stay right here.

Alex: So you are saying inspector that the weapons were found in different rooms.

Inspector: Indeed.

Michael: Can I ask what the weapons are? Could they not be weapons at all? Just something that could

be used as a weapon and left carelessly lying about?

Inspector: No sir they are definitely weapons.

Muriel: What are they then?

Inspector: Weapon number one is a Glock17. A weapon used by Marines amongst others and it is loaded with 15 rounds.

Jane: Is that a gun?

Inspector: Yes it is miss.

Michael: Ok, no argument about that. I agree it is definitely a weapon.

Inspector: Item number two is a length of thin piano wire wound onto a wooden handle at each end.

Muriel: Yuckkkk, even I can see that has got to be a weapon. And such a grisly one too. It has made me shudder.

Inspector: A very vicious weapon indeed miss, and as you can see, not some household item that has been carelessly left lying about.

Michael: ok, ok.

Inspector: Weapon number three. This is a very crude weapon indeed but a weapon none the less. It is a large gentleman's sock filled with a heavy ball bearings. Strictly speaking we should not necessarily class it as a murder weapon. It could be used to incapacitate someone but used effectively, it could certainly be used for murder.

Michael: Well there is certainly variety.

Inspector: Yes there is, which brings me to the final weapon. Which is the most curious.

Jane: In which way?

Inspector: It is a rather ordinary looking pillow, one that would be at the top of a bed.

Michael: Come now inspector, surely you can't count that as a murder weapon.

Inspector: I believe I can sir.

Muriel: But you just said it is an ordinary pillow.

Inspector: Which it is but something has been added to it which has led me to believe it was intended to smother somebody. To asphyxiate them.

Jane: What! What had been added to it?

Inspector: I did indeed carry out a very thorough search. I was very meticulous indeed. When I lifted the pillow from the bed, I turned it over and printed on the back in large bold letters, I believe made with a felt marker, was the word 'Goodbye'. I believe that would have been the last thing the victim would have seen.

Michael: I agree that is odd, possibly not a weapon but very odd.

Alex: So what are you thinking? Four different murderers, or four different victims?

Inspector: I have to ask myself, if there are four different victims does he murderer really need a different weapon for each one?

Michael: Exactly what I was thinking.

Jane: So why would there be four different weapons?

Inspector: Looking at it logically, four different murderers

Muriel: Oh my gosh!!!

Alex: Just a minute. You say there are four different weapons but what is the use of that? Where are the murderers?

Michael: Yes, there is nobody else here except for the four of us.

Inspector. Exactly sir, which is why I must ask you to remain where you are. Nobody is to leave at this point.

END OF ACT TWO
